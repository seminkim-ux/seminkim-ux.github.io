Overview

Optimal Kiosk UX for
premium brand launch.

I planned the kiosk UX for Tim Hortons Korea's launch in 2023.
Due to the short launch timeline, I planned a UX suitable for a coffee brand
based on Burger King kiosk UX that was already connected to
existing development sources. After researching kiosk UX
for beverage and coffee brands, I presented and launched the most optimal UX.

Insight

I researched the kiosk UX of major beverage and coffee brands
to identify their strengths and weaknesses.

    T Brand

  • Cart on right layout
  • 2-depth menu structure

    H Brand

  • Cart at bottom layout
  • Most diverse payment methods

    P Brand

  • Narrow cart area on right
  • Promotional video at top

    C Brand

  • Cart on right layout
  • Automatic cash receipt processing

    E Brand

  • Cart at bottom layout
  • Categories in two rows

    B Brand

  • Cart at bottom layout
  • Categories in two rows

Insight

The main menu screens are
mainly divided into two types:
cart at bottom / cart on right.

Two Layout Types

The two layout types with cart at bottom or on right
differ more by personal preference than by usability.
The right layout makes it easier to check initially added items, but can distract focus when browsing menus by dividing attention.

Insight

After selecting a menu item,
the option selection screens fall into three main types.

Case 1.

All options provided
on the first option screen

Too much content on one screen makes it hard to focus.

Case 2.

Basic options on first screen,
additional options provided only when user selects

Additional options provided by user choice
allows steps to be skipped

Case 3.

Basic options on first screen,
additional options required afterwards

Creates too many steps even when not needed.
