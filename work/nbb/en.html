Overview

Transforming online orders
into a brand's unique service.

No Brand Burger was operating online orders only through external channels.
They needed to reduce costs with their own channel and build relationships with users.
To create a rational ordering UX that aligns with the brand concept "Why pay more",
we achieved the goal of fast registration and ordering.

Process

I oversaw the entire UX planning process.

Define

  • Competitor analysis and evaluation
  • UX trend research
  • Service trend research
  • Brand analysis

I analyzed various franchise apps from a heuristic usability perspective. I researched UX and service trends, and studied brand characteristics.

Ideation

  • Co-creation workshop
  • Service Concept
  • Research on distinctive key features

Through regular workshops with the marketing department, development team, and IT department, I analyzed needs and established the concept. I defined distinctive key features unique to the service.

Prototype

  • Information Architecture
  • Wireframe creation
  • Notification message writing

I designed the overall structure, created wireframes for each detailed screen including main, registration, ordering, and payment. I wrote situation-specific notification messages to be sent as feedback.

Test

  • Test scenario creation
  • Testing with employees
  • Notification testing
  • Field testing

I created test scenarios. I coordinated between the client and developers during employee testing. I conducted tests to verify compliance with notification rules and in-store ordering tests.

Solution

The first screen designed
to quickly satisfy
various needs.

Personalization

Defined the essential elements for the home screen.
First, I placed the order button prominently on the screen.
And placed buttons for scanning QR codes
at restaurant tables for immediate ordering
and for checking membership barcodes
used at kiosks on the home screen for easy access.

• For effective explanation, I used design images,
but in reality, I created wireframes and planning documents.

Personalized service is visible on the home screen.
The Quick Order feature allows users to easily
reorder their previous orders.
Greeting messages using nicknames
and displaying the user's stamps
and coupon status at the top
delivers elements necessary
from the user's perspective.

Solution

Quick signup lowers
the entry barrier.

With SNS easy signup, users can register quickly
without entering information
just by selecting the information
provision consent button.
For direct registration, we decided to use
phone numbers as login IDs to eliminate
the struggle of choosing an ID.
We also minimized the information required
during registration. We also prepared
solutions for phone number changes.

Solution

Comfortable
ordering is
fast ordering.

By providing elements that users need during
the ordering process in advance, we lower entry barriers.
Comfortable ordering that reduces factors to consider
and elements to select directly, while still providing thorough guidance, is essential UX that creates consensus between the service and users.

1. We compare existing delivery locations with the current location and ask for confirmation to prevent errors.

2. Even when not logged in, users can experience the entire ordering process and choose member/guest ordering at the final step.

3. Users can order more easily with previous orders or pre-set quick orders.
