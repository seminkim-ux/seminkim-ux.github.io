Placing weather information on the main screen was determined by the client. However, the design drafts weren't receiving positive feedback.

I requested simpler weather icons and animated interactions. As someone who collects many references, I shared Naver Watch's weather design and effects as a reference. I asked the designer to change the large text elements like temperature display to a light font.

Providing feedback to colleagues working on different aspects is not easy.
Having done design work myself, I consider it a sensitive matter.
However, I believe there's nothing more important and valuable than receiving objective and constructive feedback.
The boundaries can be blurry, and sometimes feelings get hurt, causing hesitation, but I find teammates and stakeholders who understand the direction and can exchange feedback to be truly valuable. That seems to be the beauty of teamwork.

Content and Templates

Automation features, which operate automatically when
conditions are met, are more complex than other control features.
We also planned story-based content and
templates that users can follow directly for setup.

The automation feature was introduced in the second phase of the project.

This was because we needed to demonstrate to the parent company that we were consistently providing new features and enhancements. I wondered how users would perceive automation when even the control features from the first phase weren't familiar to them.

I determined that even if a feature has been decided, it's important not to simply provide just the functionality. If we only do what's requested, that's closer to just taking dictation. Considering users and presenting necessary content is something I believe all team members should do, not just UX designers or planners.
Apple Home and Naver Pay also provide content-based guidance screens.
For better understanding, I created content in a who+when+what format, and designed templates that users could set up with minimal changes based on these examples. The template feature was planned based on my experience working on an e-book program at my previous company, where I designed templates with usability in mind.
